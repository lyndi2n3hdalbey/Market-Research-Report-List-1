<p><p>Weakly Basic Styrene Type Anion Exchange Resins are categorized into Type I and Type II based on their functional group structure and application. Type I resins typically have a quaternary ammonium functional group, providing low pH stability, making them suitable for removing weakly acidic anions. Type II resins possess primary or secondary amines, allowing them to handle a broader range of anions, including strong acids. Both types are widely used in water treatment, pharmaceuticals, and chemical processing, offering distinct advantages in specific applications.</p></p>
