<p><p>The automotive spark and glow plugs market consists of various types tailored for different engine requirements. Hot spark plugs operate at higher temperatures for improved ignition in gasoline engines, while cold spark plugs function at lower temperatures to prevent pre-ignition in high-performance applications. Metal glow plugs, used in diesel engines, provide quick heating for efficient start-up, while ceramic glow plugs offer superior durability and temperature resistance, enhancing cold-start performance. These technologies cater to diverse automotive needs and engine specifications.</p></p>
